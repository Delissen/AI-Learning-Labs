## Stunts

Release Year: 1990

Developer: Distinctive Software

Publisher: Broderbund

Genre: Racing / Simulation

Stunts is considered one of the most innovative racing games of the DOS era. The game featured a powerful track editor that allowed players to create their own racing circuits. Players could choose from a variety of sports cars and race on tracks containing loops, jumps, corkscrews and other stunt elements.

### Opponents

The game featured four well-known computer-controlled drivers.

#### Skid Vicious

The fastest and most feared opponent in the game. Skid Vicious often set the best lap times and was considered the ultimate benchmark by experienced players.

Driving Style:

* Extremely aggressive
* Rarely makes mistakes
* Often achieves record lap times

#### Helen Wheels

A highly skilled driver known for consistency and technical precision.

Driving Style:

* Smooth cornering
* Strong on technical circuits
* Consistently competitive

#### Otto Parts

A solid all-round competitor who performs well on most tracks.

Driving Style:

* Balanced
* Reliable
* Rarely crashes

#### Bernie Rubber

Usually the easiest opponent to defeat.

Driving Style:

* Conservative
* Slower lap times
* Good opponent for beginners

### Vehicles

Available vehicles included:

* Acura NSX
* Audi Quattro Sport
* Chevrolet Corvette ZR-1
* Ferrari GTO
* Jaguar XJR9
* Lamborghini Countach
* Lamborghini LM002
* Lancia Delta Integrale
* Porsche 962
* Porsche March Indy
* Porsche Carrera 4

### Track Elements

The famous track editor included:

* Loops
* Corkscrews
* Jumps
* Elevated roads
* Slalom sections
* Tunnels
* Banked corners
* Intersections
* Drawbridges

### Legacy

Many players consider Stunts one of the greatest DOS racing games ever made because of its combination of driving physics, replay functionality and an advanced track editor. A dedicated online community still creates tracks and competitions decades after its release.


## Test Drive II: The Duel

Release Year: 1989

Developer: Distinctive Software

Publisher: Accolade

Genre: Racing

Test Drive II: The Duel was one of the most realistic driving games of its era. Players raced high-performance sports cars on public roads while avoiding traffic and police enforcement.

### Vehicles

#### Ferrari F40

Top Speed: 324 km/h (201 mph)

One of the most iconic supercars of the 1980s and often considered the fastest car in the base game.

#### Porsche 959

Top Speed: 317 km/h (197 mph)

A technologically advanced sports car with excellent handling and all-wheel drive.

#### Lamborghini Countach 5000QV

Top Speed: 295 km/h (183 mph)

Known for its aggressive styling and powerful V12 engine.

### Expansion Pack Cars

Accolade released several expansion disks introducing additional vehicles.

#### Ferrari Testarossa

Top Speed: 290 km/h (180 mph)

#### Corvette ZR-1

Top Speed: 282 km/h (175 mph)

#### Lotus Esprit Turbo

Top Speed: 257 km/h (160 mph)

#### Mazda RX-7 Turbo

Top Speed: 241 km/h (150 mph)

#### Shelby Cobra 427

Top Speed: 266 km/h (165 mph)

#### Nissan 300ZX Turbo

Top Speed: 249 km/h (155 mph)

### Locations

Expansion disks also introduced new environments:

* California Challenge
* European Challenge
* Muscle Cars Expansion
* Super Cars Expansion

### Gameplay Features

* Dashboard view
* Manual gear shifting
* Police pursuits
* Oncoming traffic
* Day and night driving
* Realistic acceleration and braking

### Legacy

Test Drive II was one of the first racing games to focus heavily on realistic driving, authentic vehicles and police chases. It became one of the defining racing games of the DOS era and inspired many later simulation-style racers.

Release Year: 1989

Developer: P.Z. Karen Co. Development Group

Publisher: California Dreams

Genre: Racing / Vehicle Management / Simulation

Street Rod combines drag racing, street racing, vehicle ownership and automotive mechanics. Players begin with a limited budget and attempt to become the top street racer by purchasing, upgrading and racing classic American cars.

## Grand Prix Circuit

Release Year: 1988

Developer: Distinctive Software

Publisher: Accolade

Genre: Formula One Racing Simulation

Grand Prix Circuit is one of the earliest Formula One racing simulators released for DOS computers. The game was designed to recreate the challenge of Formula One racing during the turbo era of the late 1980s.

Unlike arcade racing games, success required players to learn braking points, cornering techniques and the unique characteristics of each circuit.

---

## Gameplay Features

Grand Prix Circuit focused on:

* Formula One racing
* Learning real-world circuits
* Driver selection
* Car selection
* Championship competition

The game did not feature the extensive garage management or tuning systems found in games such as Street Rod.

---

## Historical Significance

Grand Prix Circuit was one of the most advanced Formula One games available on DOS computers in the late 1980s.

Players praised:

* Realistic circuit layouts
* Challenging driving model
* Distinct driver personalities
* Different vehicle characteristics

The game helped establish Accolade as one of the leading publishers of racing simulations during the DOS era.
